About

You were not
born who you are.
You became it.

TSG is not a platform. It is a position. A refusal to accept that who you are was decided for you — by biology, by family, by the gaze of others.

"

One is not born, but rather becomes, a woman.

Simone de Beauvoir — The Second Sex, 1949

Beauvoir said it about women. We say it about everyone. Identity is not a given. It is a construction — ongoing, contested, and always in progress.

The Subordinate Gaze is an archive built on that premise. We collect the work of people who are actively constructing who they are — through their answers, their questions, their contradictions.

We don't offer answers. We offer the right prompts.

01

We question the given

Nothing about your identity is fixed. Not your gender, your class, your nationality, your name. Everything you call "you" is a sediment of decisions — yours and others'. We start from there.

02

We build in public

The archive is not a finished product. It grows with every person who contributes. Your response to an assignment becomes part of the collective record — anonymously, permanently, honestly.

03

We refuse comfort

The assignments are not therapeutic exercises. Some of them will be uncomfortable. That discomfort is the point. You cannot construct a self without first dismantling the one you were handed.
