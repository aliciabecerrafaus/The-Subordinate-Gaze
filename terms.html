How it works

You are anonymous.
Your answer is not.

TSG works because no one knows who you are — but everyone can read what you wrote. That asymmetry is the point.

The Anonymous Principle

Your name stays
with you.
Your truth doesn't.

When you submit a response to an assignment, it enters the archive without your name, your face, or any identifying information. What remains is only the answer — raw, unperformed, unfiltered by the need to manage how you're perceived.


Anonymity is not cowardice here. It is the condition that makes honesty possible. You can say what you actually think because no one will know it was you who thought it.

"My mother always said I looked like her sister. Not her — her sister. I spent years trying to figure out what that meant. I think I still am."

1

No account required

You do not need to register, sign in, or verify anything. You arrive, you respond, you leave. The archive holds your words — not your identity.

2

Responses are permanent

Once submitted, a response becomes part of the archive. We do not delete them. This is not a draft — it is a deposit. Think before you write. Then write anyway.

3

No replies, no likes

There is no engagement mechanic. No one can react to your answer, reply to it, or rate it. The archive is not a conversation — it is a collective monologue. Everyone speaks. No one responds.

4

One response per assignment

You get one shot at each prompt. This is not because we don't trust you — it's because the first honest answer is almost always the right one.

5

Honesty over eloquence

We are not curating beautiful writing. We are collecting honest thinking. A single sentence matters more than a polished paragraph. Say what you actually mean.

6

No hate. No performance.

Responses that target other people, perform outrage, or use the archive as a platform will be removed. This is the only rule we enforce. The rest is yours.

What we
promise you.

We built TSG because we believe the question "who am I?" deserves more than a personality test or a therapy session. It needs a place — an archive, a record, a proof that others asked the same thing and left their answer behind.

We will never sell your data. We will never attach your name to your words. We will never use the archive for anything other than what it is: a collection of honest human answers to hard human questions.

Our commitments

  • Your responses are anonymous and will remain so, always.
  • We do not store IP addresses linked to submissions.
  • The archive is read-only to the public — no profiles, no threads.
  • We will never monetise your responses or use them for training models.
  • If TSG ever closes, the archive will be made publicly available as a static record.
  • We are two people. Not a startup. Not a brand. This matters.
